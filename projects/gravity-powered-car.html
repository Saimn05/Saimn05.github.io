Gravity-Powered Car (Recycled Materials)

Overview

Objective: build an ultra-low-cost gravity car to study how mass distribution and axle alignment affect run distance and speed down an inclined track.

Materials & fabrication

  • Body: carved styrofoam or recycled plastic sheet; shaped to reduce frontal area.
  • Wheels: plastic food-container lids; axles made from metal rods with bushings (or ball bearings if available).
  • Ballast: metal block placed in the internal cavity to tune center of mass (sketch 2).

Method

Used a simple jig to ensure parallel axles. Per configuration ran 5 test runs, recorded distances and times, and averaged results. Iterated ballast position and wheel alignment.

Results

Small shifts in ballast (5–10 mm) affected distance by up to ~6%. Axle misalignment >1° caused wobble and added rolling resistance. Recommendations: use bearings and a stiffer axle mount for improved repeatability.
